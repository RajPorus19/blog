## The Aryan invasion myth

Western education often teaches that **Aryans invaded India** and brought civilization. This "Aryan myth" was constructed from scratch by European orientalists in the 19th century.

## How it started

In 1786, **Sir William Jones**, a British judge in India, noticed similarities between Sanskrit, Greek and Latin. He proposed a **mother language** — Indo-European. So far, nothing shocking: that's serious linguistics.

The problem came later. **Max Müller** and other orientalists turned this linguistic discovery into a **racial theory**. Their reasoning:

1. Sanskrit is close to European languages
2. Therefore Sanskrit speakers ("Aryans") are our cousins
3. They must have "brought" civilization to India
4. The darker-skinned southerners (Dravidians) are "inferior" conquered peoples

## Colonial agenda

This theory wasn't innocent. It served **political objectives**:

| Objective | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Legitimize domination** | If Brits and Indian "Aryans" share origins, colonization is almost a "homecoming" |
| **Divide and rule** | Creating an Aryan vs Dravidian opposition prevents anti-colonial unity |
| **Racialize castes** | Upper castes = "Aryan", lower castes = "Dravidian" — justifying hierarchy |

## What modern genetics says

Ancient DNA studies (Narasimhan et al., 2019, Science) have refuted this theory:

- **No brutal invasion** — migrations were gradual over centuries
- **Indus ancestry dominates** — 60-80% of all Indians' ancestry comes from the Indus Valley Civilization
- **No racial boundary** — there's a continuous genetic gradient from north to south, not a break

## Dravidian erasure

While Sanskrit was elevated to "Latin of India", Dravidian languages and cultures were systematically devalued:

- **Tamil** — a living classical language with 2500+ years of continuous literature — was treated as a "dialect"
- **Indus Valley Civilization** (Harappa, Mohenjo-daro) — far older and more advanced than Vedic culture — was minimized
- **Siddhars** and **Siddha medicine** — ancient Tamil science — ignored

## Why it matters today

This orientalist bias continues to influence:
- History textbooks
- Identity politics in India
- Indian studies in the West
- The debate on Indus script (undeciphered, but likely Proto-Dravidian)

**Best way to counter this bias?** Read Dravidian sources, study Tamil history, and always question colonial narratives.
